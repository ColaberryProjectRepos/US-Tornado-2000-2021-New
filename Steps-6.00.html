2.Recommendations for Mitigating Tornado Fatalities in States Prone to Higher Fatality Rates
South and Midwest regions had significantly higher average fatality rates for tornadoes from 2000 to 2021, with a 96% increase in the South and an 88% increase in the Midwest. Alabama, Missouri, Tennessee, Mississippi, and Texas witnessed a substantial surge of 373% in average fatalities. Urgent targeted tornado preparedness efforts are needed in these regions to enhance safety measures.
  • Enhancing Tornado Alerts in High-Risk States: Identify high-risk states and strengthen alert systems like sirens, weather radios, and mobile alerts  for better effectiveness and reach. Ensure timely dissemination for effective sheltering and preparedness.
  • Effective Tornado Preparedness through Collaborative Efforts: Engage with local authorities, emergency management agencies, meteorological services, and government organizations to develop and implement effective tornado response plans, strategies, and timely tornado watches alerts.
  •  Enhancing Tornado Resilience and Emergency Response: Strengthen building codes for tornado-resistant structures and safe rooms. Improve coordination among emergency response agencies, conduct drills, and refine response plans for efficient assistance during tornado events.
